Emergency Plans

Eden House maintains comprehensive emergency and contingency plans to ensure the safety and continuity of care for all residents in any emergency situation — including natural disasters, infrastructure failures, disease outbreaks, or other crises.

Our emergency plans are reviewed and updated regularly in consultation with management, staff, and regulatory authorities. They cover evacuation procedures, communication protocols, alternate care arrangements, and essential service continuity.

These plans comply fully with the requirements of the Long-Term Care Homes Act and the Retirement Homes Act.

View Emergency Plans Questions? Contact Us

Visitor Policy

Eden House warmly welcomes visits from family members, friends and community members. Our visitor policy is designed to balance the important benefits of social connection for residents with the need to protect the health and safety of our entire community.

The current visitor policy outlines entry requirements, screening procedures, visiting hours and expectations for conduct during visits. Requirements may vary depending on current health directives from the Ministry of Long-Term Care or public health authorities.

We recommend reviewing the current policy and, when in doubt, calling ahead to confirm any active requirements before visiting.

CQI Report & Action Plan

Our Continuous Quality Improvement (CQI) Report and Action Plan documents Eden House's performance across key care indicators, identifies areas for improvement, and outlines concrete actions to address them.

CQI is a fundamental part of how Eden House operates — not just a regulatory requirement, but a genuine commitment to ongoing improvement in resident care, staff practices, and facility operations.

The report is reviewed regularly by management and the Board, with progress tracked and reported transparently.

Privacy Policy

Eden House collects, uses and discloses personal and personal health information in accordance with the Personal Health Information Protection Act (PHIPA) and all other applicable Ontario privacy legislation.

Our privacy policy explains what information we collect, why we collect it, how it is used, who may have access to it, and how it is protected. Residents and families have the right to access their own records and to request corrections.

If you have a privacy concern or complaint, please contact our Privacy Officer directly through the information on our Contact page.

AODA Multiyear Accessibility Plan

Eden House is committed to accessibility for all persons, including those with disabilities. Our AODA Multiyear Plan outlines our commitments under the Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act, 2005, and the Integrated Accessibility Standards Regulation.

The plan addresses customer service, information and communications, employment, and the built environment — describing our current practices and our plan to continuously improve accessibility for residents, staff, families and visitors.

View AODA Plan

Fire Safety Plan

Eden House maintains a comprehensive Fire Safety Plan for the protection of all residents, staff and visitors. The plan was most recently updated September 2024 and covers fire prevention measures, detection systems, evacuation procedures, staff training requirements and more.

Fire safety drills are conducted regularly to ensure all staff are fully prepared to respond to any fire emergency calmly and effectively.
