Infection Prevention
& Control

Protecting our residents, staff and visitors through evidence-based IPAC practices, transparent policies, and full compliance with provincial health guidelines.

Our Commitment

Resident Safety
Is Our Priority

Eden House takes infection prevention and control seriously. Our IPAC program is built on current best practices, Ministry of Long-Term Care directives, and the guidance of public health professionals.

All policies are reviewed and updated regularly. Key documents are publicly available below. If you have questions about our protocols, please don't hesitate to contact us.

✓ Ministry of Long-Term Care Compliant ✓ RNAO Best Practice Guidelines ✓ RHRA Standards ✓ Regular Audits

Personal Protective Equipment

Rigorous hand hygiene policies enforced for all staff, residents and visitors throughout the facility at all times.

🌡️

Active Screening Protocols

🔬

Outbreak Management

Clear outbreak identification, reporting and management procedures aligned with public health authority requirements.

Quality Improvement Reviews

IPAC Practices

How We Protect
Our Community

Every aspect of our daily operations is designed with infection prevention in mind.

🏥

Environmental Cleaning

Thorough, frequent cleaning and disinfection of all resident areas, common spaces, high-touch surfaces and equipment using Ministry-approved products and schedules.

💉

Vaccination Programs

We support and facilitate annual vaccination programs for residents and staff, including influenza and other provincially recommended immunizations.

🎓

Staff Education

All staff receive ongoing IPAC education and training, including new hire orientation, annual updates, and outbreak-specific refreshers.

🚿

Droplet & Contact Precautions

Droplet and contact precaution protocols are in place and immediately activated when a resident has a suspected or confirmed transmissible illness.

📊

Surveillance & Reporting

Active surveillance for infection rates with mandatory reporting to public health authorities and the Ministry of Long-Term Care as required.

🤝

Family Communication

Families are promptly notified of any health concerns affecting their loved ones, and are kept informed throughout any outbreak period with regular updates.

For Families & Visitors

Visiting Eden House

We warmly welcome visits from families and friends. To keep our residents safe, we ask all visitors to follow our current protocols.

📋

Review the Visitor Policy

Please read our current visitor policy before your visit. It outlines any active requirements for entry, screening, and conduct during your visit.

🩺

Health Screening

Visitors may be asked to complete a health screening upon arrival. If you are feeling unwell, please postpone your visit and contact us to reschedule.

🧼

Hand Hygiene

Please use the hand sanitizer provided at the entrance and throughout the building. This simple step protects our most vulnerable residents.

😷

Masking Requirements

Masking requirements vary depending on current outbreak status and provincial guidance. Check our current visitor policy or call ahead to confirm.

📞

Call Ahead

We recommend calling ahead to confirm visiting hours and any current protocols before making the trip. Our office is open Monday–Friday, 8:30 am – 4:30 pm.

❓

Questions Welcome

Our staff are always happy to answer questions about our IPAC practices. Please don't hesitate to ask at reception or call us directly.
