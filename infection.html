Infection Prevention & Control

Keeping our residents
safe & protected

Eden House follows rigorous infection prevention and control protocols to protect the health of all residents, staff, and visitors at all times.

Our Approach

A proactive, science-based
IPC program

Eden House's infection prevention and control program follows all provincial guidelines and the directives issued by Public Health Ontario, the Ministry of Long-Term Care, and the Retirement Homes Regulatory Authority. Our dedicated IPC lead coordinates policy, education, and response at every level of the organization.

🧼

Hand Hygiene

Rigorous hand hygiene protocols for all staff, residents, and visitors, supported by hand sanitizer stations throughout the facility.

😷

PPE Protocols

Appropriate personal protective equipment is worn by staff whenever indicated, in line with the latest provincial guidance.

🔬

Surveillance & Testing

Active monitoring for respiratory and other illnesses, with rapid response protocols to contain any outbreak quickly.

💉

Immunization

Annual influenza vaccination campaigns for residents and staff, and facilitation of other recommended immunizations.

🏠

Environmental Cleaning

Enhanced cleaning and disinfection of high-touch surfaces and resident rooms — daily and between all residents.

📋

Staff Education

Ongoing IPC training and education for all team members, ensuring consistent practices across every shift.

Visitor Information

Visiting a loved one

We welcome family and friends to visit residents. Visitor policies may vary based on current provincial directives and any active outbreak situations. Please contact us before your visit to confirm current visitor guidance so your trip goes smoothly.
