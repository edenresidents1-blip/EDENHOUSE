About Eden House

A community cornerstone since 1968 — providing compassionate, person-centred long-term care and retirement living in the heart of Wellington County.

Our Story

A Home Built on
Care & Community

Eden House Care Facility is a community-oriented 58-bed nursing home and 20-bed retirement home nestled in the countryside between Rockwood, Eden Mills and Guelph, Ontario. Surrounded by lush grounds and a peaceful Memorial Garden, we have been a pillar in our community for over 55 years.

Together with our staff, professional associates and dedicated volunteers, we strive every day to give our residents the best possible care — making Eden House truly "a nice place to live."

Eden House is licensed by the Ministry of Long-Term Care and the Retirement Homes Regulatory Authority (RHRA), and is affiliated with the Registered Nurses Association of Ontario (RNAO) as a Best Practice Spotlight Organization.

Our History

55 Years of Caring

From our founding in 1968 to today, Eden House has grown and evolved — always with the same core commitment to resident-centred care.

1968

Eden House Founded

Eden House Care Facility opens its doors in Wellington County, nestled between the communities of Rockwood, Eden Mills and Guelph — beginning a legacy of community-centred care.

1990s

Expansion & Growth

Eden House grows its capacity and service offerings, adding the Retirement Home wing and expanding programming to meet the evolving needs of residents and the community.

2000s

RNAO Best Practice Affiliation

Eden House becomes affiliated with the Registered Nurses Association of Ontario (RNAO) as a Best Practice Spotlight Organization — embedding evidence-based nursing care across all programs.

2020

COVID-19 Response

Eden House implements comprehensive infection prevention and control protocols to protect residents and staff during the global pandemic, maintaining safety and communication with families throughout.

2024

Continued Excellence

Eden House renews its Fire Safety Plan, updates its BPSO Statement, and continues to invest in staff, programming and resident experience — maintaining its reputation as one of the region's most trusted care homes.

Now

A Community Institution

Today Eden House continues to be a vibrant, caring home for 78 residents — guided by its founding values and a dedicated team of staff and volunteers committed to making every day meaningful.

What Guides Us

Mission, Vision
& Values

Every decision at Eden House flows from a clear set of principles that place residents and their families at the centre of everything we do.

Our Mission

Eden House is committed to providing exemplary long-term care services to Residents. Our focus is on the provision of individualized care that respects, supports and enables Residents to be as independent as possible.

Our Vision

Eden House strives to be a leader in the long-term care field, recognized for high quality care and service and its commitment to working in partnership with Residents and their families. We strive to design and deliver programming that meets the growing and changing needs of Residents and the community we serve, and to provide a rewarding and empowering work environment.

Our Accreditation

RNAO Best Practice
Spotlight Organization

Eden House is proudly affiliated with the Registered Nurses Association of Ontario (RNAO) as a Best Practice Spotlight Organization (BPSO). This designation reflects our commitment to implementing RNAO's evidence-based Best Practice Guidelines across our care programs.

Being a BPSO means our nurses and care staff use the best available research and evidence to guide their practice — resulting in better outcomes for residents and a stronger, more knowledgeable care team.
